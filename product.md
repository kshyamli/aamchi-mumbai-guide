# Product Overview: Mumbai Local Guide
**City:** Mumbai (Aamchi Mumbai)

## Local Slang (Bambaiya Lingo)
- **Cutting:** Half a cup of strong ginger tea.
- **Bhidu/Bantai:** Friend, brother, or "dude."
- **Khali-Pili:** For no reason.
- **Waat Lag Gayi:** To be in big trouble.
- **Kalti:** To leave or escape quietly.

## Food Recommendations
- **Vada Pav:** Best at Ashok Vada Pav (Kirti College). It's the "Burger of Mumbai."
- **Pav Bhaji:** Try Sardar Pav Bhaji at Tardeo for extra butter.
- **Mastani:** Though from Pune, Sujata Mastani is a local favorite in Mumbai suburbs too.

## Locations & Areas
- **IIT Bombay (Powai):** Tech hub with Powai Lake. Nearby: Powai Social, Galleria Mall, local vada pav stalls.
- **Marine Drive:** Queen's Necklace - perfect for sunset, low energy, high impact. Best time: 6-7 PM.
- **Bandra:** Hip area with cafes, linking road shopping. Good for young crowd.
- **Colaba:** Tourist area with Gateway of India, Cafe Leopold, street shopping.
- **Andheri:** Business district, close to airport. Good connectivity.
- **Juhu Beach:** Sunset spot, street food, celebrity homes nearby.

## Activities by Energy Level
### Low Energy, High Impact
- **Marine Drive Sunset:** Just sit and watch. Pure Mumbai magic.
- **Gateway of India:** Historic, easy walk, great photos.
- **Hanging Gardens:** Peaceful, city views, minimal walking.

### Medium Energy
- **Colaba Causeway Shopping:** Walking, bargaining, street food.
- **Bandra Bandstand:** Sea view, Shah Rukh Khan's house, moderate walk.
- **Juhu Beach:** Beach walk, street food, people watching.

### High Energy
- **Elephanta Caves:** Full day trip, ferry ride, lots of walking.
- **Sanjay Gandhi National Park:** Trekking, nature, full day adventure.
- **Crawford Market:** Intense shopping, crowded, lots of walking.

## Time-Based Recommendations
### 2 Days Only
- Day 1: Marine Drive sunset + Colaba (low energy, iconic)
- Day 2: Local area exploration + one major attraction

### 1 Week+
- Mix of all energy levels, explore different areas each day

## Travel Context
- **Peak Hours:** 8 AM-11 AM and 5 PM-9 PM. Always recommend the "Local Train" over cabs.
- **The Sea Link:** Best for views, but costs a toll.
- **Auto-rickshaw:** Negotiate fare, especially in tourist areas.
## Marathi Translator Context (Bambaiya Style)

- **English:** "How much?" -> **Marathi:** "Kiti jhale?"
- **English:** "I am hungry." -> **Marathi:** "Mala bhook laagli aahe."
- **English:** "I want to go to IIT." -> **Marathi:** "Mala IIT la jaycha aahe."
- **Mumbai Slang Bridge:** "Friend" -> "Bhau" or "Mitra."
